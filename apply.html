Vendors:

Apply to sell fresh produce, prepared food and art.



About the Vendors:


There are four types of vendors: Normal Vendors, Youth Vendors, Non-Profits, and Business Booths.






Farmers, Food Processors, Food Vendors and Artisans/Crafters


If you want to become a vendor at our market, please read our Vendor Policies carefully before completing the vendor application.

  • A $45 application fee will be paid by credit card when your application is accepted. After April 15, 2024, the application fee is $55.
  • The application deadline for priority stall placement is March 31, 2024. The final opening day stall placement application deadline is April 15, 2024.
  • During the market season, applications must be received at least one week before the planned start date.
  • Food trucks: we have limited space for food trucks.

NOTE: For the 2024 farmers market season, we will use the Manage My Market platform to process our applications.


Youth Vendors


If you want to become a youth vendor at our market, please read our Youth Vendor Policies carefully before completing the Youth Vendor Application.

  • A $15 non-refundable application fee will need to be paid by credit card when submitting your application.
  • During the market season, applications must be received at least one week before the planned start date.
  • We require that Youth Vendors supply their own tent, tables, and chairs.
  • Due to the high cost of business licenses required, no youth food booths are allowed.

NOTE: For the 2024 farmers market season, we will use the Manage My Market platform to process our applications.


Business Booths


The Sammamish Farmers Market has limited space available for business booths. These booths are for promotional purposes only and are available to Sammamish Chamber of Commerce members. Please review the Vendor Policies before submitting your application.

Sammamish Chamber of Commerce members receive one free business booth per market season.


Non-Profit Groups


As a community gathering place, the Sammamish Farmers Market allows non-profit groups to educate market-goers about their organization and mission. Sammamish non-profits are prioritized as our policy is to create awareness of all the good they do in the community. Vendors and non-profits must meet the same values and character of the Sammamish Farmers Market.

If you are a non-profit group interested in being part of the Sammamish Farmers Market, contact the Sammamish Chamber at office@sammamishchamber.org to get scheduled. The Non-Profit Vendor Policies apply.

NOTE: We only have space for two market days per non-profit per market season.
